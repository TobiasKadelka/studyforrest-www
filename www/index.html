High-resolution, ultra-highfield (7T) fMRI data

Possibly the largest consecutive sample of brain imaging data recorded during natural language processing. FMRI data corrected for EPI distortions.

Rich stimulus

Participants listened to a narrated, audio-only version of the two-hour Hollywood movie "Forrest Gump". The stimulus is aligned to the commercial DVD release to facilitate further annotation.

High-resolution anatomical images

T1 and T2-weighted anatomical images with a voxel-size of 0.7 mm for all participants.

Time-of-flight angiography

High-resolution blood vessel localizer for the field-of-view of the functional brain scans.

Versatile noise estimates

Simultaneous measurements of cardiac and respiratory trace during fMRI recording. Motion estimates extracted from fMRI data prior distortion correction. Independent evaluation of technical noise with a full-length phantom recording.

Data overview

The data release contains a wide variety of structural brain scans and a large amount of functional brain imaging data for each of the 20 participants. Neuroimaging data is accompanied by measurements of physiological and technical noise, as well as stimulus annotations.

Inter-subject response similarities. Distributed activation patterns and voxel timeseries.

BOLD time series correlation and pattern similarity estimated from representational similarity analysis on anatomically aligned data reveal consistent brain responses to quasi natural auditory stimulation across all participants. Areas of maximum similarity in both hemispheres are centered on the anterolateral portion of the Planum Temporale — part of the auditory ventrolateral pathway and known to be involved in processing complex sounds and speech.

About this project

This project is an open invitation to anyone to participate in a decentralized effort to explore the opportunities of open science in neuroimaging. It aims to document how much (scientific) value can be generated out of a single data release by publication of scientific findings derived from this dataset, algorithms and methods evaluated on this dataset, and/or extensions of this dataset by acquisition and integration of new data.

This website is maintained by the Psychoinformatics lab at the Otto-von-Guericke University Magdeburg, Germany.
