Cortical surface reconstruction
Tissue segmentation and 3D meshes

Structural brain scans can be used to reconstruct the cortical surface of the brain. This process estimates various properties of the brain, such as thickness of the gray matter, or cortical curvature. The example image below shows both hemispheres of an individual brain. For the left hemisphere the outer ("pial") surface is shown. For the right hemisphere the white matter surface is shown with a colored overlay indicating the cortical curvature at each surface point.

Usage: Left-click and drag to rotate the view, middle-click to pan, and right-click (or scroll) to zoom in and out.

Technical details

These data were generated from the T1 and T2-weighted images of each participant using the Freesurfer 5.3 image segmentation and cortical surface reconstruction pipeline. More information on Freesurfer.
