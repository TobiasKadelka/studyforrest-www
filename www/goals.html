Project goals

Rich data acquisition

Studying the complex interplay of concurrent cognitive processes requires adequate data.

Apart from providing detailed and multi-faceted structural brain scans the primary focus of this project lies on massive acquisition of functional brain imaging data during natural stimulation. All collected data will be curated and made publicly available to enable independent researchers from a broad range of disciplines to study the brain's natural behavior in a rich environment.

Re-conceptualization

Beyond peak activation locations.

Despite strong anatomical similarities, when two brains experience the same sensory stimulation their activity patterns, as measured with fMRI, will be different. This is due to fine-scale inter-individual differences in anatomical and functional organization of the brain. But with prolonged natural stimulation, for example with movies, we could argue that brain activity patterns observed in two individuals at a particular time in the movie nevertheless capture, to some degree, the same state of a large variety of cognitive processes in the brain. We tend to feel the similar emotions at the same time, we tend to remember the same story facts, and we share common reflexes, such as to look at peoples faces, or to be surprised by sudden loud sound effects (see Hasson et al. The neuroscience of film, 2008 for more information).

In other words, the temporal dynamics of changes in the state of our brains are similar when we watch the same movie. Mathematically speaking, the shape of the brain state trajectory in a high-dimensional voxel-space can serve as a metric to describe distributed, idiosyncratic brain activity dynamics. We can investigate similarities in the brain state trajectories across individuals to identify common patterns. For example, Haxby et al (2011) have developed an algorithm that uses these similarities to transform brain activity patterns from one person to another via a common reference space of brain function. This project aims to enable large-scale evaluation of such algorithms, in order to facilitate their application in clinical diagnosis.

Data re-use and extension

We invite anyone to use this dataset.

The present data versatility enables many applications. Here are a few idea what could be done:

  • The fMRI data is a prolonged sample of brain response patterns from sound and speech processing, and applicable to address questions of language and speech processing, but also music perception, auditory attention and emotion. All in the context of rich naturalistic stimulation.
  • Stimuli richness of the dataset lends itself to explore questions of how the brain encodes semantic representations throughout the cortex.
  • Many of the neuroscientific questions can be easily rephrased in machine learning terms such as prediction or classification (for example: how one can predict gender of the voice the participant was listening to from the brain response patterns at any particular moment).
  • It could be used as a free reference resource for more technical applications, including but not limited to benchmarking existing algorithms, or developing new methods for: anatomical or functional inter-subject alignment, physiological de-noising, segmentation and blood vessel detection, brain parcellation, or large networks analysis.
  • Any part of the dataset can be used as a free resource to develop educational materials: for online and offline workshops, web-tutorials, or excercises in text books.
  • It may stimulate the development of multi-modal visualization or exploration tools that could be used in education or to make neuroimaging data more accessible to other disciplines.
  • As a complex reference test case to capture data processing provenance for a large variety of analysis procedures and software tools.

Moreover, an attempt was made to enable independent researchers to extend the present dataset in order to open up even more research questions. All data acquisition procedures, stimulation software, and conversion scripts were made publicly and freely available, including a reference implementation for dataset access that contains basic tests of dataset integrity.
