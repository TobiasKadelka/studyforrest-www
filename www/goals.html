Ideas

Thepresent data versatility enables many applications. Here are a few idea what could be done:

  • The fMRI data is a prolonged sample of brain response patterns from sound and speech processing, and applicable to address questions of language and speech processing, but also music perception, auditory attention and emotion. All in the context of rich naturalistic stimulation.
  • Stimuli richness of the dataset lends itself to explore questions of how the brain encodes semantic representations throughout the cortex.
  • Many of the neuroscientific questions can be easily rephrased in machine learning terms such as prediction or classification (for example: how one can predict gender of the voice the participant was listening to from the brain response patterns at any particular moment).
  • It could be used as a free reference resource for more technical applications, including but not limited to benchmarking existing algorithms, or developing new methods for: anatomical or functional inter-subject alignment, physiological de-noising, segmentation and blood vessel detection, brain parcellation, or large networks analysis.
  • Any part of the dataset can be used as a free resource to develop educational materials: for online and offline workshops, web-tutorials, or excercises in text books.
  • It may stimulate the development of multi-modal visualization or exploration tools that could be used in education or to make neuroimaging data more accessible to other disciplines.
  • As a complex reference test case to capture data processing provenance for a large variety of analysis procedures and software tools.

Moreover, an attempt was made to enable independent researchers to extend the present dataset in order to open up even more research questions. All data acquisition procedures, stimulation software, and conversion scripts were made publicly and freely available, including a reference implementation for dataset access that contains basic tests of dataset integrity.

Let us know

If you use this dataset for any purpose, or publication. Let us know at info@studyforrest.org so we can add a reference to your work to this website.
